03 — The AC Premium

Here, AC is not
comfort.

It is the condition of economic survival in a 45°C city. Those who can afford it keep working. Those who can't stop.

The worker income loss is money that was never earned. The AC premium is money actually spent — paid to SOMELEC every month, before any revenue, before any profit. It is the bill heat sends directly to the commercial sector.

In Nouakchott, air conditioning is not a displayed luxury. It is a class marker as real as a postal code. Shops and offices that can afford a unit stay open in the afternoon. The others pull the shutters or slow down. Heat separates those who can afford to work from those who cannot.
