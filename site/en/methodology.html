Methodology

How we measured the heat tax

Every assumption is declared. Every source is cited. Every limitation is named before you find it. This analysis is descriptive — we measure, we do not assert causation.

Core principle

Measurement, not model.

This analysis makes no causal claim. We do not say heat causes underdevelopment. We say: at these temperatures, these workers produce less. That is a measurement, not an inference.

No regressions. No identification strategy. Just hourly data, an international standard, and transparent arithmetic.

All estimates are lower bounds unless stated otherwise. When an assumption can go either way, we choose the more conservative one.

02 — The ILO/ISO curve

We didn't invent this curve.

The work capacity curve comes from the ILO and ISO 7243:2017. We apply it at moderate intensity (200–300 W metabolic rate) — representative of market vendors, construction workers, and port workers.

# ILO/ISO curve — temperature °C → capacity %
24°C → 100%   28°C → 88%   30°C → 80%
32°C → 70%   34°C → 50%   36°C → 40%
38°C → 30%   40°C → 20%   42°C → 10%
# Linear interpolation between points
capacity(T) = interp(T, ILO_ISO_curve)

Assumption

Moderate intensity for all three target professions.

Reason: standing work with light loads — ISO 7243 moderate classification.

Alternative: heavy intensity (heavy construction) would show more compression. Moderate is the central, conservative choice.

ILO (2019). Working on a Warmer Planet. Geneva: ILO. ISBN 978-92-2-132968-9.

ISO 7243:2017. Ergonomics of the thermal environment — Assessment of heat stress using the WBGT index. Geneva: ISO.

03 — Compression rate

Effective capacity = hourly sum.

For each hour in 2019–2025, we apply the curve to the observed temperature. The sum gives the effective capacity.

# Monthly effective capacity
cap_effective = Σ capacity(temp_h)
              for h in working_hours_of_month

# Monthly compression
compression% = (total_hours − cap_effective)
                ÷ total_hours × 100

# Annual average 2019–2025
compression = 20.0%  ·  capacity = 80.0%

The result is stable across the full period — between 78.3% and 82.4%. This is not an artifact of an exceptional year.

04 — Wage anchor

SMIG: the lower bound.

Monetary estimates are anchored to Mauritania's minimum wage (SMIG: 3,000 MRU/month, ILO ILOSTAT 2022). Using the minimum wage understates the real loss — informal workers often earn more.

# Hourly derivation
SMIG monthly = 3,000 MRU
SMIG hourly = 3,000 ÷ 26 days ÷ 8h = 14.42 MRU/h

# Three wage scenarios
conservative = 14.42 MRU/h (1.0× SMIG)
central = 20.19 MRU/h (1.4× SMIG)
upper = 25.96 MRU/h (1.8× SMIG)
ILO ILOSTAT. Dataset EAR_INEE_CUR_NB_A.
Mauritania, monthly minimum wage, 2022. Value: 3,000 MRU.

05 — AC premium

Money actually spent.

Unlike the income loss, the AC premium is money physically paid to SOMELEC. Commercial stock estimated from the JICA 2018 Master Plan, Table I-14.

# Commercial stock (JICA 2018)
pure_commercial = 952 ha ÷ 120 m² = 79,333 units
mixed_use_GF = 1,270 ha × 0.15 × 30% ÷ 120 m² = 4,763
govt_offices = 204 ha × 0.15 ÷ 30 m² = 10,200
total = 94,296 spaces

# AC penetration: 1 in 3
ac_units = 94,296 × 1/3 = 31,432

# Annual cost (central scenario)
cost = units × 2.5 kW × 0.75 × hours × 5.903 MRU/kWh

Key assumption

1 in 3 commercial spaces has AC.

Reason: local knowledge — most Nouakchott commercial buildings have AC in the owner's office or back room, not full coverage.

Alternative: 1 in 5 (conservative) to 1 in 2 (upper). All three scenarios are reported.

JICA (2018). Nouakchott City Urban Master Plan. Table I-14.
openjicareport.jica.go.jp/pdf/12324729.pdf

SOMELEC. Tarifs Basse Tension. Codes 7106–7136. Rate: 5.903 MRU/kWh.

06 — Shadow output

This is not GDP.

The 80% figure is not a national accounts measure. It is a scalar applied to observed temperature data. The total heat tax combines two independent components: worker income loss and commercial cooling expenditure.

# Total heat tax
income_loss = 120,000 × 14,742 MRU = 1,769M MRU
ac_premium = 31,432 units × consumption × rate = 869M MRU
────────────────────────────────────────────
total = 2,638M MRU ≈ $73M USD / year

07 — Limitations

What this analysis does not say.

We name our limitations before you find them.

What this analysis does not claim

  • Does not claim heat causes lower GDP
  • Does not predict future losses or climate change impacts
  • Does not cover agriculture or fishing sectors
  • Does not represent all workers — outdoor informal sector only
  • The capacity curve applies to moderate intensity only
  • OSM commercial stock is a lower bound (~15% of true stock)
  • SMIG 2022 may have been updated — all monetary estimates are lower bounds
  • AC penetration rate (1 in 3) is a local estimate, not a direct measurement
  • Does not account for seasonal workers leaving Nouakchott in summer

These limitations bound the analysis without invalidating it. Every estimate points in the same direction: the real heat tax is probably higher than what we report, not lower.
