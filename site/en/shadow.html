The total heat tax

Two independent components — one cause

Worker income loss120,000 workers × 14,742 MRU/year · central scenario 1,769M MRU
Commercial AC premium31,432 units × consumption × 5.903 MRU/kWh · SOMELEC 869M MRU
Total heat taxLower bound · central scenario 2,638M MRU
≈ $73M USD / year

These two components are independent — there is no double counting. The income loss hits outdoor workers who slow down under heat. The AC premium is paid by businesses that can afford to stay open. Different populations, different mechanisms, one cause.

The result — 2,638 million MRU, roughly $73 million — is a lower bound. OSM commercial stock covers only 15% of the true total. SMIG is the legal floor, not the real wage. Agricultural and fishing workers are not counted. The real heat tax is higher.

In October, the city loses 391 million MRU in a single month.

That is 14.8% of the annual tax concentrated in 31 days. For a worker earning SMIG, October alone erases almost a full month's wage.

All values are 2019–2025 averages. They are stable year to year — heat in Nouakchott is not an event. It is a permanent condition.

The 80% figure is not a spectacular conclusion. It is simply what the data gives when you look at it honestly. No regression, no heroic assumptions, no general equilibrium model. An international curve, six years of temperature, transparent arithmetic.
